# Real Estate Marketing Page Audit
**Page:** https://olympiamarketing.com/real-estate-marketing-advertising/
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Perspective:** CEO of a real estate brokerage in Southwest Florida

---

## Executive Summary

The page communicates that Olympia Marketing has real estate experience and offers relevant services, but it falls short of what a brokerage CEO would need to see before making a buying decision. The page lacks concrete proof, has weak CTAs, misses major SEO opportunities, and does not speak directly to the pain points of brokerage leadership. Below are detailed findings and recommendations.

---

## 1. CONVERSION ISSUES

### 1.1 No Lead Capture on the Page
- **Problem:** Every CTA links to `/contact/` — a generic contact page. There is no on-page form, no lead magnet, no consultation scheduler. A brokerage CEO evaluating vendors will not click through to a generic form; they will compare you against competitors who make it easier.
- **Fix:** Add an inline form in the hero or mid-page with fields like Name, Brokerage Name, Number of Agents, Phone, Email. Offer a free "Real Estate Marketing Audit" or "Competitive Analysis" as the incentive.

### 1.2 Weak CTA Copy
- **Problem:** CTAs say "Contact Us Today" and "Contact Olympia Marketing Now to Start Your Journey to Success." These are generic and do not convey value.
- **Fix:** Use benefit-driven CTAs:
  - "Get Your Free Brokerage Marketing Plan"
  - "See How We Grew [Client Name]'s Listings by 125%"
  - "Book a 15-Minute Strategy Call"

### 1.3 Broken CTA Link
- **Problem:** The "Contact Us Today" button in the "Real Estate Marketing & Advertising Done Right" section links to `http://v` — a broken URL. This is a critical bug that kills conversions outright.
- **Fix:** Correct the href to `/contact/` or the inline form anchor immediately.

### 1.4 No Conversion Path Segmentation
- **Problem:** A solo agent, a team leader, and a brokerage CEO all have different needs and budgets. The page treats them all the same.
- **Fix:** Add a short section or toggle: "I'm a Solo Agent | I Lead a Team | I Run a Brokerage" that tailors the messaging or routes to appropriate service tiers.

### 1.5 No Pricing Signals
- **Problem:** Zero indication of investment level. Brokerage CEOs are busy and want to quickly assess if this is in their budget range.
- **Fix:** Add "Plans starting at $X,XXX/mo" or "Typical brokerage engagement: $X,XXX–$XX,XXX/mo" — even a range removes friction.

---

## 2. SEO ISSUES

### 2.1 Title Tag is Weak
- **Current:** "Real Estate Marketing by Olympia Marketing"
- **Problem:** Does not include geographic modifiers or value proposition. Misses local intent entirely.
- **Fix:** "Real Estate Marketing & Advertising | Southwest Florida | Olympia Marketing" or "Real Estate Marketing Agency for FL Brokerages | Olympia Marketing"

### 2.2 Meta Description is Generic
- **Current:** "Olympia Marketing offers real estate marketing services to boost your revenue. Get effective SEO, content, and social media strategies. Contact us now!"
- **Problem:** No differentiation, no local signal, no specificity.
- **Fix:** "Olympia Marketing helps Southwest Florida brokerages and agents grow listings, leads, and closings with SEO, PPC, and full-service digital marketing. See our 125% average growth results."

### 2.3 Focus Keyword Mismatch
- **Current focus keyword:** "Real Estate Marketing & Advertising" (includes ampersand — not how people search)
- **Fix:** Target "real estate marketing agency" or "real estate marketing services Florida" as primary. Use "real estate advertising" and "realtor marketing" as secondary.

### 2.4 Missing Local SEO Signals
- **Problem:** The page content never mentions Southwest Florida, Fort Myers, Naples, Cape Coral, Bonita Springs, Estero, or any Lee/Collier County geography. For a brokerage CEO searching "real estate marketing agency Fort Myers," this page will not rank.
- **Fix:** Naturally weave in SWFL geographic terms throughout the copy. Add a section: "Serving Real Estate Professionals Across Southwest Florida" with city names.

### 2.5 No FAQ Schema
- **Problem:** The page has no FAQ section at all, unlike other service pages on the site (e.g., the Marketing & Advertising parent page has FAQs). FAQ schema is a major SERP real estate opportunity.
- **Fix:** Add 5-7 FAQs targeting long-tail queries:
  - "How much does real estate marketing cost?"
  - "What marketing strategies work best for real estate brokerages?"
  - "How long does SEO take for real estate websites?"
  - "Do you work with individual agents or only brokerages?"
  - "What ROI can I expect from real estate digital marketing?"

### 2.6 Thin Service Descriptions
- **Problem:** The bottom services section (Content Marketing, Blogging, On-site Optimization, etc.) is entirely generic SEO content — nothing real-estate-specific. It reads like it was copied from the general SEO page.
- **Fix:** Every service description should reference real estate use cases. E.g., for Blogging: "We create neighborhood guides, market update posts, and buyer/seller resource content that positions your brokerage as the local authority."

### 2.7 Missing Internal Links
- **Problem:** No links to related service pages (SEO, PPC, Web Design, Social Media) or to real estate case studies/blog posts.
- **Fix:** Add contextual internal links throughout the copy and a "Related Services" section at the bottom.

## 3. TRUST SIGNALS

### 3.1 Client Logos Without Context
- **Problem:** Three client logos are shown (KnowledgeBase FL, John R. Wood, Premier Plus) but with no context — no case study links, no results, no testimonials. Just logos floating on the page.
- **Fix:** Under each logo, add a one-line result: "Helped John R. Wood increase organic leads by X% in 6 months." Link each to a full case study.

### 3.2 The 125% Stat is Unsupported
- **Problem:** The page claims "Our average real estate practice sees an increase of 125% in business in the first year." This is a bold claim with zero supporting evidence — no methodology, no sample size, no timeframe details. A skeptical CEO will dismiss this.
- **Fix:** Support it with specifics: "Based on X clients from 2022-2025, measured by [metric]." Better yet, break it into a mini case study with before/after numbers.

### 3.3 No Testimonials
- **Problem:** Not a single quote from a real estate client. This is a massive gap. A brokerage CEO wants to hear from peers.
- **Fix:** Add 2-3 testimonials from brokerage owners or team leaders with full name, title, company, and headshot. Video testimonials would be even stronger.

### 3.4 No Case Studies
- **Problem:** The page mentions working with brokerages and agents but provides zero case studies or detailed results.
- **Fix:** Add at least one detailed case study section: "How We Helped [Brokerage] Increase Closings by X% in Y Months" with the challenge, strategy, and measurable results.

### 3.5 No Industry Credentials or Affiliations
- **Problem:** No mention of NAR (National Association of Realtors) familiarity, MLS integrations, IDX experience, or real estate tech stack knowledge (KVCore, BoomTown, Chime, Follow Up Boss, etc.).
- **Fix:** Add a section showing familiarity with real estate platforms and industry organizations. This signals you actually understand the business.

### 3.6 No Team/Expertise Showcase
- **Problem:** No information about who will actually work on the account — no bios, no credentials, no "meet your strategist."
- **Fix:** Feature 1-2 team members with real estate marketing experience, including their background and photo.

---

## 4. CTA & FUNNEL ISSUES

### 4.1 Single Funnel Stage Only
- **Problem:** The page only targets bottom-of-funnel visitors (those ready to contact). It ignores the research and comparison stages of the buyer journey.
- **Fix:** Add a downloadable resource ("The 2026 Real Estate Marketing Playbook") for mid-funnel leads, capturing emails for nurture sequences.

### 4.2 No Phone Number on Page
- **Problem:** The phone number (239-308-4011) is in the header but not in the page body. A CEO who scrolls past the hero and is ready to call has to scroll back up.
- **Fix:** Include the phone number near every major CTA, especially in the "Ready to Supercharge" section.

### 4.3 No Urgency or Scarcity
- **Problem:** Nothing on the page creates urgency. A brokerage CEO can visit, think "maybe later," and never return.
- **Fix:** Add: "We work with a limited number of brokerages per market to avoid conflicts of interest" or "Free strategy sessions available this month."

### 4.4 No Social Proof Near CTAs
- **Problem:** The CTAs sit alone without reinforcement. No "Join X brokerages we've helped" or "Rated 5 stars on Google" near the buttons.
- **Fix:** Place a trust badge or micro-testimonial adjacent to every CTA button.

---

## 5. INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC IMPROVEMENTS

### 5.1 No Understanding of Brokerage Business Model
- **Problem:** The copy never addresses the unique challenges of a brokerage CEO: recruiting agents, retaining top producers, competing with national franchises, managing brand across dozens of agents, compliance with real estate advertising rules (FREC/DBPR in Florida).
- **Fix:** Add a section: "We Understand the Brokerage Business" that addresses recruiting support, agent retention marketing, brand compliance, and multi-agent campaign management.

### 5.2 No Mention of Real Estate Tech Stack
- **Problem:** Zero mention of IDX/MLS integration, CRM platforms (KVCore, Sierra Interactive, Chime, BoomTown, Follow Up Boss), Zillow/Realtor.com optimization, or listing syndication.
- **Fix:** Add a "Technology Partners & Integrations" section showing logos and descriptions of real estate platforms you work with.

### 5.3 Services List is SEO-Heavy, Missing Key Channels
- **Problem:** The services listed are almost entirely SEO-related (Content Marketing, Blogging, On-site/Off-site SEO, Keywords). Missing critical channels for real estate:
  - Social media (Instagram/Facebook/TikTok for listings and agent branding)
  - Video marketing (property tours, agent intro videos, drone footage)
  - Listing photography/staging coordination
  - Open house marketing
  - Retargeting/remarketing campaigns
  - Zillow/Realtor.com profile optimization
  - Google Business Profile management
  - Review generation
- **Fix:** Expand the services section to reflect the full scope of real estate marketing needs, not just SEO.

### 5.4 Copy Tone is Too "Marketing Agency"
- **Problem:** Phrases like "turbo-charge," "skyrocket," "unmatched talent and experience," and "IS OUR SUGGESTIONS ACTUALLY GOING TO INCREASE BUSINESS?" (grammatically incorrect, appears to be leftover from informal writing) feel unprofessional and salesy. A brokerage CEO managing millions in transactions wants a professional partner, not hype.
- **Fix:** Adopt a more consultative, data-driven tone. Replace superlatives with specifics. Fix the grammar issue in the "Key To Effective" section.

### 5.5 No Competitor Differentiation
- **Problem:** The "Why Choose Olympia Marketing" section lists "Broad Industry Experience," "National Footprint," and "Full-Service Digital Solutions" — all of which any agency could claim. Nothing specific to real estate.
- **Fix:** Differentiate with real estate-specific advantages: "Deep knowledge of Florida real estate regulations," "Experience across residential, commercial, and luxury segments," "We've managed campaigns during both seller's and buyer's markets."

### 5.6 Missing Seasonal/Market Awareness
- **Problem:** No mention of how marketing strategy adapts to Southwest Florida's seasonal market (snowbird season, summer slowdown), hurricane season considerations, or current market conditions.
- **Fix:** Add content showing you understand market cycles and adjust strategy accordingly. This is what separates a real estate marketing expert from a generalist.

## 6. CONTENT & COPY ISSUES

### 6.1 Grammar and Typos
- "IS OUR SUGGESTIONS ACTUALLY GOING TO INCREASE BUSINESS?" — should be "ARE OUR SUGGESTIONS"
- The "Full-Service Digital Solutions" bullet point is cut off mid-sentence: "...to meet your real estate" (missing the end)
- "Olympia Marketings" — missing apostrophe, should be "Olympia Marketing's"
- Multiple instances of inconsistent capitalization

### 6.2 Redundant Content
- The "Contact Us Today" CTA appears three times with similar surrounding copy. Each instance should have unique supporting text.
- The hero section mentions SEO specifically but the page is supposed to cover all marketing services.

### 6.3 No Visual Proof
- Only two images referenced: a generic real estate agent image and a brokerages/realtors stock photo. No screenshots of campaigns, dashboards showing results, website designs built for clients, or social media examples.
- **Fix:** Add visual proof — campaign screenshots, Google Analytics before/after, website mockups, social media post examples.

## Bottom Line

As a brokerage CEO, I would not contact Olympia Marketing based on this page alone. The page tells me you do real estate marketing but does not prove it. It lacks the specificity, social proof, and industry depth I need to trust you with my brokerage's brand and budget. The broken link, grammar issues, and generic copy signal carelessness — which is the opposite of what I want in a marketing partner.

The good news: the foundation is there. Three named real estate clients, a bold growth stat, and relevant service offerings give you something to build on. The fixes above, particularly adding testimonials, case studies, local SEO signals, and an on-page lead form, would dramatically improve this page's ability to convert a decision-maker like me.
