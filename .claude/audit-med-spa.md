# Med Spa Page Audit: Olympia Marketing
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Perspective:** CEO of a luxury medical spa in Southwest Florida evaluating Olympia Marketing as a potential agency partner

---

## Executive Summary

The med-spa page at olympiamarketing.com/med-spa/ has solid foundational content but reads more like a marketing textbook than a pitch to a sophisticated med spa owner. As a med spa CEO spending $10K-$30K/month on marketing, I need proof, specificity, and confidence that this agency truly understands my business. The page currently does not close that gap.

---

## 1. Content Improvements to Increase Conversions

### Problem: The page talks ABOUT med spa marketing rather than PROVING it

**Current state:** The page opens with "We've empowered Medical Spas of all sizes to expand their reach, attract new clients, and elevate their brand presence in a competitive market." This is a claim with zero evidence.

**What would make me want to hire them:**

- **Lead with a specific result.** Replace the generic hero text with something like: "We helped a Bonita Springs med spa grow from 0 to [X] monthly bookings in their first year." The Physician Aesthetic Institute / Bonita Springs MedSpa case study exists as a blog post but its results are buried -- they need to be front and center on this page.

- **Show revenue impact, not marketing metrics.** Med spa CEOs care about patient bookings, average ticket value, and revenue per treatment room. The GBP performance chart showing clicks and impressions is nice, but I want to see: "Client went from 40 to 120 monthly bookings" or "Average patient lifetime value increased 35%."

- **Name the treatments you have marketed.** The page lists "skincare treatments, laser hair removal, Botox, dermal fillers" -- these are the most basic offerings. A luxury med spa CEO wants to see that you understand CoolSculpting, PRF/PRP, IV therapy, hormone optimization, Morpheus8, microneedling, chemical peels, PDO threads, etc. Naming advanced treatments signals genuine industry knowledge.

- **Address the actual pain points of a med spa CEO.** The page never mentions:
  - Patient no-show rates and how marketing automation reduces them
  - The challenge of filling non-peak hours (Tuesday mornings, etc.)
  - Membership/subscription model marketing (70%+ of medspa clients are repeat customers per industry data)
  - Compliance challenges with advertising Botox/fillers (FDA and platform restrictions)
  - The medical director / injector personal branding challenge

### Problem: "Take Action Now" section contains WRONG copy

**Critical bug:** The "Take Action Now for Exponential Growth" section reads: "Ready to take your **saas marketing** to the next level? Join forces with Olympia Marketing today to see your **property sales, tenant engagement**, and overall brand awareness skyrocket."

This is clearly copy-pasted from the SaaS or real estate page. This alone would make me close the tab. It signals carelessness and undermines every claim of "industry expertise."

### Problem: Broken CTA link

The "Contact Us Today" button under the "Medical Spa Marketing & Strategy Executed to Perfection" section links to `http://v` -- a broken URL. This is another trust-destroyer.

## 2. SEO Improvements

- **Meta description:** "Medical spa marketing that works! Olympia Marketing offers SEO, social media, and web development to grow your client base. Contact us now!"
- **Focus keyword:** (empty -- no focus keyword is set)

### Recommendations

**A. Set a focus keyword and optimize around it.**
Primary keyword should be: `med spa marketing agency` or `medical spa marketing`
Secondary keywords to target:
- "med spa SEO"
- "med spa social media marketing"
- "medical spa advertising agency"
- "medspa marketing company"
- "med spa marketing agency Florida" (local modifier)
- "medical spa digital marketing"

**B. Rewrite the title tag.**
Current: "MedSpa Marketing: Olympia Marketing's Proven Strategies"
Suggested: "Med Spa Marketing Agency | SEO, Social Media & Growth for MedSpas"
- Includes primary keyword at the front
- Describes services
- Under 60 characters

**C. Rewrite the meta description.**
Current: "Medical spa marketing that works! Olympia Marketing offers SEO, social media, and web development to grow your client base. Contact us now!"
Suggested: "Olympia Marketing is a med spa marketing agency in Southwest Florida specializing in SEO, social media, and patient acquisition strategies. We helped a Bonita Springs medspa grow 100% in year one."
- Includes specific result (social proof in SERP)
- Geographic signal
- Primary keyword included
- Under 160 characters

**D. Heading structure needs work.**
Current H-tag hierarchy:
- H1: "Medical Spa Marketing & Advertising Solutions"
- H2: "Medical Spa Marketing & Advertising" (redundant with H1)
- H3: "Ready to Elevate Your Medical Spa Marketing?"
- H2: "Why Choose Olympia Marketing for Your Medical Spa?"
- H3: Industry Expertise / Tailored Marketing Strategies / Proven Track Record / Comprehensive Services
- H3: "Medical Spa Marketing & Strategy Executed to Perfection"
- H2: "Medical Spa Marketing Strategies"
- H4: (individual strategies)

Issues:
- H1 and first H2 are nearly identical -- wasted keyword opportunity
- The H4 tags under "Marketing Strategies" should be H3s for proper hierarchy
- No H2/H3 targeting long-tail keywords like "med spa SEO strategy" or "social media for medical spas"

**E. Missing structured data.**
The page should have:
- LocalBusiness or ProfessionalService schema
- FAQ schema (add an FAQ section with common med spa marketing questions)
- Review/AggregateRating schema if testimonials are added

**F. Internal linking is weak.**
The page links to:
- /contact/ (multiple times)
- Blog posts about GBP optimization and Bonita Springs case study
- Beauty industry category archive

Missing internal links:
- Link to the /medical-spa-research/ page (detailed industry data exists but is not linked from the main service page)
- Link to individual service pages (SEO, social media, web development) with med-spa-specific anchor text
- Link to any portfolio/case study page

**G. Content length is thin for a competitive keyword.**
Top-ranking med spa marketing agency pages typically have 2,000-3,000+ words of substantive content. This page has substantial word count but much of it is generic filler. The content needs to be denser with specific, unique information.

## 3. Missing Content That Would Build Trust

### No client testimonials on the page
The live site has a testimonials slider in the template, but there are no med-spa-specific testimonials visible. A luxury med spa CEO needs to hear from other med spa owners -- not generic business testimonials.

**Add:**
- 2-3 named testimonials from med spa owners/managers (with photo, name, spa name, location)
- Specific quotes mentioning measurable results: "Olympia helped us increase our Botox bookings by 40% in Q1"

### No case study detail on the page
The Bonita Springs MedSpa blog post exists but the actual results are vague even in that post. The service page should have an embedded mini case study:
- Client name and location
- Starting situation (new practice, no online presence, etc.)
- Services provided (SEO, GBP optimization, social media, website)
- Results with specific numbers over a defined timeframe
- Before/after screenshots of rankings, traffic, or GBP insights

### No before-and-after marketing examples
Show actual work product:
- Screenshot of a med spa website you built
- Example social media posts/campaigns
- GBP profile optimization before vs. after
- Email campaign examples with open rates

### No industry data or thought leadership
The /medical-spa-research/ page has excellent industry data ($21B market, 12% CAGR, 70% repeat customers, Gen Z/Millennial trends). NONE of this appears on the main med-spa service page. Incorporating 3-4 data points would immediately elevate credibility:
- "The U.S. medspa industry is projected to surpass $21 billion by 2025"
- "Over 70% of medspa clients are repeat customers"
- "Millennials and Gen Z make up nearly 50% of new medspa visits"

### No pricing transparency or engagement model
Med spa CEOs want to know: What does this cost? What is the engagement model? Even a general pricing tier or "starting at" range would reduce friction. At minimum, describe:
- Monthly retainer model vs. project-based
- What a typical first 90 days looks like
- Minimum engagement period

### No mention of compliance expertise
Medical spas face unique advertising restrictions:
- FDA rules on promoting off-label uses of injectables
- Platform-specific ad policies (Meta restricts before/after photos for cosmetic procedures)
- HIPAA considerations for testimonials and patient photos
- State medical board advertising regulations

Mentioning compliance awareness would be a significant differentiator.

## 4. CTA Placement and Copy Suggestions

### Current CTAs
1. "Get Started" -> /contact/ (hero section)
2. "Contact Us Today" -> /contact/ (after intro)
3. "Contact Us Today" -> http://v (BROKEN -- after "Strategy Executed to Perfection")
4. "Contact Olympia Marketing Now to Start Your Journey to Success." -> /contact/ (after "Take Action Now")

### Issues
- **CTA #3 is broken** (links to `http://v`)
- **CTA #4 is absurdly long** for a button
- **All CTAs go to a generic /contact/ page** -- no med-spa-specific intake form
- **No mid-page conversion opportunity** (e.g., "Download our Med Spa Marketing Guide" or "Get a Free Med Spa Marketing Audit")
- **No urgency or specificity** -- "Contact Us Today" tells me nothing about what happens next

### Recommended CTA Strategy

**Hero CTA:** "Get Your Free Med Spa Marketing Audit" -> dedicated landing page or form with med-spa-specific questions (number of locations, monthly marketing budget, current patient volume, top 3 treatments)

**Mid-page soft CTA (after marketing strategies section):** "Download: The 2025 Med Spa Marketing Playbook" -> gated PDF lead magnet capturing email. This lets prospects who are not ready to call still enter the funnel.

**After case study section:** "See How We Grew a Bonita Springs MedSpa by [X]% in 12 Months" -> link to detailed case study

**Bottom CTA:** "Schedule a 15-Minute Strategy Call" -> Calendly or booking link. Med spa CEOs are busy. Telling them it is 15 minutes reduces commitment anxiety. Remove "Start Your Journey to Success" -- it is cliche.

**Sticky mobile CTA:** A floating "Book a Call" button on mobile would significantly improve conversion for the 60%+ of visitors on phones.

## 6. Additional Issues Found

### Inconsistent terminology
The page alternates between "Medical Spa," "Med Spa," and "MedSpa." Pick one primary term for SEO consistency (recommend "med spa" as it has the highest search volume) and use it consistently in headings and body copy. Use variations naturally in supporting text.

### Images are generic stock photos
The hero image is a stock photo of "a Caucasian woman talking" (per the image filename: `depositphotos_334684762-stock-photo-photo-of-caucasian-woman-talking.webp`). This should be replaced with:
- Photos of actual med spa environments
- Screenshots of campaign results
- Photos of the Olympia team working with med spa clients

### Page loads client logos but few are med-spa-related
The client logos section shows Knowledge Base Real Estate, Quality Service Company, FrogPad Outdoor Storage, Florida Blockchain Business Association, etc. Only "Physician Aesthetic Institute" and "Alexander Academy" are beauty/wellness related. If this page is targeting med spa CEOs, show only beauty/wellness clients or create a med-spa-specific client section.

### No FAQ section
Adding a FAQ section would:
- Capture long-tail keyword traffic ("how much does med spa marketing cost," "do I need SEO for my med spa," etc.)
- Enable FAQ schema markup for rich snippets
- Address objections directly on the page

Suggested FAQs:
- How much does med spa marketing cost?
- How long before I see results from SEO for my med spa?
- Do you handle social media content creation or just strategy?
- Can you help with Google Ads for Botox and fillers?
- Do you work with med spas outside of Florida?
- What makes marketing a med spa different from other businesses?

## Bottom Line: Would I Hire Them?

As a luxury med spa CEO in Southwest Florida, this page would NOT convince me to reach out. Here is why:

- The copy-paste error ("saas marketing" / "property sales") signals sloppiness
- The broken link signals neglect
- The lack of specific med spa results makes "proven track record" ring hollow
- The generic stock photo and boilerplate language do not differentiate from the 50 other agencies that claim to "specialize" in med spas
- There is no indication they understand the compliance complexities of marketing medical aesthetic services

However, the underlying content structure is solid, and the blog posts suggest real med spa experience. With the fixes outlined above -- especially the case study, real testimonials, compliance messaging, and corrected copy -- this page could be very effective. The bones are good; the execution needs significant refinement.
