# Roofing Contractor Marketing Page Audit

**URL:** https://olympiamarketing.com/roofing-contractor-marketing/
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Perspective:** Owner of a roofing company in Southwest Florida evaluating this page before hiring a marketing agency

---

## Overall Impression

The page communicates the right message at a high level -- it speaks to real pain points roofing contractors face and promises lead generation. However, as a roofing company owner spending real money, I see significant gaps in proof, specificity, and conversion architecture that would make me hesitate before reaching out.

---

## 1. CONVERSION ISSUES

### 1a. Weak Call-to-Action Strategy
- **Only CTA is "Contact Us Today" linking to /contact/** -- a generic page. There is no inline form, no phone number click-to-call in the hero, no "Get a Free Marketing Audit" offer. A roofer in decision mode wants to act NOW, not navigate to another page and figure out a general contact form.
- **No urgency or offer.** CTAs like "Contact Us Today" are passive. Compare to: "Get Your Free Roofing Marketing Audit -- See Where You're Losing Leads" or "Book a 15-Minute Strategy Call."
- **No sticky CTA or floating button.** On mobile especially, once you scroll past the hero, there is no persistent way to take action.
- **No phone number on the page body.** The number 239-308-4011 is in the site header/footer but not in the page content itself. Roofers are phone-first people -- the number should be prominent, large, and click-to-call in the hero and after key sections.

### 1b. No Lead Magnet or Low-Commitment Entry Point
- There is no downloadable resource (e.g., "The Roofing Contractor's Guide to Dominating Google in Your Area"), no free audit tool, no ROI calculator. Roofers who are not ready to buy today have zero reason to stay engaged.

### 1c. No Pricing Signals or Investment Framing
- A roofing company owner wants to know ballpark investment. Even a range ("Most roofing clients invest $2,000-$5,000/month") or a "packages starting at" signal reduces anxiety. Right now there is zero pricing context, which can feel like a red flag or a waste-of-time concern.

---

## 2. TRUST SIGNAL GAPS

### 2a. Testimonial Mismatch
- The page includes the quote: "Olympia filled our classes and changed the way we do business." This is clearly NOT from a roofing contractor -- it sounds like an education or fitness client. Using a mismatched testimonial on an industry-specific page actively hurts credibility. A roofer reading this thinks: "They don't actually have roofing clients."
- **Fix:** Add 2-3 named testimonials from actual roofing contractors with company name, location, and ideally a headshot or logo. If no roofing-specific testimonials exist yet, remove the quote entirely rather than use an irrelevant one.

### 2b. No Named Case Studies or Clients
- The "Key Wins" section lists impressive stats (200% more calls, 1% to 15% conversion) but they are completely anonymous. Which roofing company? What market? What timeframe? Without attribution, these read as made-up marketing claims.
- **Fix:** Turn at least one win into a mini case study: "ABC Roofing in Naples, FL went from 12 leads/month to 38 leads/month in 60 days. Here's how."

### 2c. No Roofing Industry Credentials
- No mention of familiarity with roofing-specific platforms (CompanyCam, JobNimbus, Roofr, AccuLynx, GAF/Owens Corning programs).
- No mention of understanding storm/hurricane season marketing cycles -- critical in SWFL.
- No mention of insurance restoration marketing, which is a massive segment of Florida roofing.
- **Fix:** Add a section or callouts showing you understand the roofing business beyond generic "local SEO and PPC."

### 2d. No Logos, Badges, or Partner Signals
- No Google Partner badge, no Facebook Marketing Partner badge, no BBB rating, no industry association memberships. These are table stakes for an agency page targeting a skeptical audience.

### 2e. The "1% to 15% Conversion Rate" Stat Appears Twice
- Once in the services intro area and again in "Key Wins." Repeating the exact same stat makes it look like thin proof being stretched.

## 3. SEO ISSUES

### 3a. Missing Focus Keyword
- The `focus_keyword` field in pages.json is empty (""). This should be "roofing contractor marketing" or "roofing marketing agency."

### 3b. Title Tag and Meta Description
- **Title:** "Roofing Contractor Marketing Services | Olympia Marketing" -- decent but could be stronger. Consider: "Roofing Contractor Marketing | Get More Roofing Leads | Olympia Marketing"
- **Meta Description:** Solid but could include a geographic signal: "...Olympia Marketing in Southwest Florida helps roofers..."

### 3c. Thin Heading Structure
- H1: "Roofing Contractor Marketing That Gets You Leads" -- good.
- H2s: "Is Your Roofing Business Struggling...", "Our Roofing Marketing Services", "Why Roofing Contractors Choose Us", "Key Wins", "FAQ" -- decent keyword distribution.
- **Missing:** No H2/H3 targeting long-tail queries like "how to get more roofing leads," "roofing SEO services," "roofing PPC management," or "roofing website design." These are missed ranking opportunities.

### 3d. No Internal Links to Related Content
- The page links only to /contact/. No links to the SEO service page, the PPC service page, the local SEO page, the reputation management page, or any blog posts about roofing marketing. This is a major missed opportunity for both SEO (internal link equity) and user experience (supporting the buyer's research journey).

### 3e. No Schema Markup Beyond Basic
- No FAQ schema for the FAQ section (easy win for rich snippets).
- No Service schema.
- No LocalBusiness schema reinforcement.
- No Review/AggregateRating schema.

### 3f. Image SEO
- Only 5 images total. The hero image is "Roofing-Image.png" -- filename is okay but generic. The other 4 are icon_67.png, icon_1.png, icon_2.png, icon_66.png -- terrible for image SEO. These need descriptive filenames and alt text.

### 3g. No Content Depth for Topical Authority
- At roughly 600 words of body content, this is thin for a competitive keyword. Top-ranking roofing marketing pages typically have 1,500-2,500 words covering specific services, process details, market insights, and FAQ depth.

## 4. INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC IMPROVEMENTS (SWFL ROOFING)

### 4a. No Storm/Hurricane Season Content
- Southwest Florida roofing is dominated by storm damage and insurance claims. The page says nothing about:
  - Marketing during and after hurricane season
  - Insurance restoration lead generation
  - Emergency/storm damage campaign capabilities
  - Seasonal marketing strategy adjustments
- This is the single biggest gap. Any SWFL roofer reading this page will wonder if the agency understands their actual business cycle.

### 4b. No Mention of Specific Roofing Services You Can Market
- The page is generic. It should call out that you can help market: roof replacements, roof repairs, roof inspections, storm damage restoration, tile/shingle/metal roofing, commercial roofing, gutter services, etc. This shows industry knowledge and also captures long-tail SEO value.

### 4c. No Geographic Specificity
- The agency is based in Estero, FL (18300 Dykes Road) but the page reads like it could be for any roofer in any state. For a local agency, leaning into SWFL market knowledge is a differentiator:
  - "We understand the Lee County, Collier County, and Charlotte County roofing market"
  - "We know how Florida homeowners search for roofers after hurricane season"
  - Mention familiarity with Florida building codes, permit marketing, etc.

### 4d. No Competitive Differentiation
- Why should a roofer choose Olympia over the dozens of agencies that specialize in roofing marketing nationally (Roofer Marketers, Roofing Marketing Pros, etc.)? The page doesn't address this. Possible angles:
  - Local SWFL agency = face-to-face meetings
  - Deep understanding of Florida roofing market specifically
  - Full-service (not just SEO or just ads)

### 4e. No Process/Timeline Section
- Roofers are practical people. They want to know: What happens after I call? How long until I see results? What do I need to provide? A simple 3-4 step "How It Works" section would reduce friction enormously.

---

## 5. CONTENT AND COPY ISSUES

### 5a. Pain Points Section is Good but Lacks Resolution Pairing
- The 6 bullet points about struggles are relatable. But they just sit there -- each pain point should be paired with or followed immediately by how Olympia solves it.

### 5b. Services Section is Surface-Level
- "Local SEO & Google Maps -- Rank higher for 'roof repair near me'" is one line. A roofer evaluating agencies wants more: What does your SEO process look like? Do you optimize my Google Business Profile? Do you build local citations? Do you create location pages for each city I serve? One sentence per service is not enough.

### 5c. FAQ Section is Thin
- Only 5 questions, and the answers are 1-2 sentences each. Expand to 8-10 questions and give substantive answers. Add questions like:
  - "What marketing channels work best for roofing contractors?"
  - "How do you handle seasonal slowdowns in roofing marketing?"
  - "Can you help me market insurance restoration services?"
  - "Do you work with roofing companies outside of Florida?"
  - "What reporting will I receive?"

## 6. TECHNICAL / UX NOTES

- **Page renders via catch-all `[...slug]/page.tsx` with `dangerouslySetInnerHTML`** -- content is stored as a single HTML blob in pages.json. This limits the ability to add interactive elements (calculators, inline forms, tabbed service details) without restructuring. Consider building this page as a dedicated component rather than rendering raw HTML.
- **No `page_type` field visible in the JSON for this entry** -- which means it may fall into the "general" layout. Industry pages get the `IndustryLayout` with case study grids. This page should be typed as "industry" to benefit from that enhanced layout.
- **No `related_categories` field** -- means the sidebar will show no related blog posts, missing a chance to keep visitors engaged and demonstrate thought leadership.

## BOTTOM LINE

As a SWFL roofing company owner, this page tells me Olympia does marketing for roofers, but it does not convince me they *understand* my business. The mismatched testimonial, anonymous stats, zero storm-season awareness, and lack of Florida-specific knowledge would make me keep shopping. The bones are there -- the pain points are real, the services listed are right -- but it needs deeper proof, stronger CTAs, and roofing industry depth to convert a skeptical contractor who has been burned by agencies before.
